Job roles

Learn about the different roles and responsibilities of your colleagues at HM Land Registry.

Head of Design

A Head of Design provides a creative vision not just for the design team but the whole organisation. They establish processes and practices for realising that vision, and set the bar for quality.

They contribute to the development of brand definition and experience principles, and ensure that those are appropriately interpreted through the teamâ€™s work.

Interaction Designer

An interaction designer works out the best way to let users interact with services, in terms of both overall flow and at the level of individual design elements.

Read the GOV UK Guidance for this role.

Content Designer

Content designers make things easier for people to understand and use. This can involve working on a single piece of content or on the end-to-end journey of a service to help users complete their goal and government deliver a policy intent. In this role your work may involve the creation of, or change to, a transaction, product or single piece of content that stretches across digital and offline channels.

Read the GOV UK Guidance for this role.

Service designer

Service designers design the end-to-end journey of a service. This helps a user complete their goal and government deliver a policy intent. In this role, your work may involve the creation of, or change to, transactions, products and content across both digital and offline channels provided by different parts of government.

Read the GOV UK Guidance for this role.

Service owner

A service owner is accountable for the quality of their service. In this role, you will be expected to adopt a portfolio view, managing end-to-end services that include multiple products and channels.

Read the GOV UK Guidance for this role.

Frontend Developer

A frontend developer designs, builds and improves website software that meets user needs. They ensure that users of the website or service can easily interact with it.

Read the GOV UK Guidance for this role.

Backend developer

A back-end developer is someone who builds and maintains the technology needed to power the components which enable the user-facing side of the website to exist. Their back end code adds utility to everything the front-end developer creates.

User researcher

User researchers plan, design and carry out research activities with users that help teams get a deep understanding of the people that use government services. This research informs policy, proposition, service, content and interaction design so that services work well for users and achieves policy intent.

Read the GOV UK Guidance for this role.

Project Manager

Project manager's are accountable for shaping and delivering projects/ programmes to quality, budget and time tolerances. This is achieved through regular reviews with the wider project team and project documentation, including verifying feature delivery actuals.

Product Manager

A product manager is responsible for the quality of their products. In this role, you will be expected to use your knowledge of user needs and business goals to frame problems and set priorities for your delivery teams.

Read the GOV UK Guidance for this role.

Product owner

Product owner's are responsible for setting the release roadmap and maximising the value of delivery within time and budget constraints. Through engagement and collaboration with stakeholders and team, they build a joint understanding of business problems and user challenges to guide the creation and ongoing maintenance of the product backlog. They are accountable for the backlog and ensure that the team has the highest value items refined and understood, ready for development.

Business analyst

Business analysts understand and analyse user and business needs.

Read the GOV UK Guidance for this role.

Delivery manager

A delivery manager is accountable for the delivery of products and services.

Read the GOV UK Guidance for this role.

Scrum master

Scrum master's are Agile subject matter experts and coach teams, product owners and stakeholders in the adoption of Agile principles, processes and techniques and enact positive change to teams. They support the product owner and team in the creation and maintenance of the backlog, including backlog refinement and facilitation of Agile events.

Test analyst

Test analysts create and execute test cases, ensuring software is tested to the right levels and that any defects, risks or issues are reported in a timely manner. They ensure all testing and defects are carried out in accordance with testing standards.

Line Manager

A line manager is a person with direct managerial responsibility for a particular employee.

Buddy

A buddy is assigned to new starters at HM Land Registry. They help to answer questions or give guidance where needed.
