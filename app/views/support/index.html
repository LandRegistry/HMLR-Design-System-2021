Support

Find out more information about the purpose, use and maintenance of the HM Land Registry Experience Library.

Why does HM Land Registry have their own Experience Library?

While the GOV.UK Design System should be a key point of reference for designers and developers at HM Land Registry, it is not specifically catered to providing components and patterns that help fulfil the needs of HMLR service users. Alongside the GOV.UK Design System, the HMLR Experience Library seeks to be a useful additional resource for the design and development communities at HMLR.

Having approved, ready-to-use components, patterns and styles available through the HMLR Experience Library will help to standardise design across all HMLR services to ensure a consistent look and feel.

Who is the HMLR Experience Library aimed at?

By hosting a range of resources and information, the HMLR Experience Library seeks to be a useful resource for the design, development and user research communities.

For example:

  • Information about getting started at HMLR is available for new designers in the Handbook.
  • Interaction designers, developers and user researchers can take a look at the experimental components and patterns that have been added.
  • All designers can refer to the accessibility section for guidance.
