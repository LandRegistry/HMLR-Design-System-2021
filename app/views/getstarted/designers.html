Getting started

Designers

If you are new HM Land Registry, we recommend working through the step-by-step guide below.

  1. Step 1 Welcome to HM Land Registry

    Welcome to the Team, by now the Head of Design, would have introduced you to everyone via Slack (What’s Slack?).

    Where to begin:

    • Read the Welcome document.
    • Get to know your Line Manager. Ask them anything you want as they're here to help you.
    • Your Line Manager will:
      • Assign you a Buddy
      • Give you access to Slack (if you haven't already)
      • Give you access to Mural (What’s Mural?)
      • Give you access to the Design Community Trello board (What’s Trello?)
      • Give you access to PluralSight tutorials (What’s PluralSight?)
    • If you haven't already, join the following Slack channels:
      • #accessibility
      • #announcements
      • #design-content
      • #frontend
      • #general
    • Attend your first Daily Design Community Catchup (9am - 9.30am, Mon - Fri)
    • Attend your first Weekly Design Community Session (1pm, Thur)
    • Check with the Head of Design that you've been invited to the Design Practice Monthly Town Hall meeting
    • Read the Hybrid Working Agreement

  2. Step 2 Introduction to work at HM Land Registry

    At HM Land Registry there are a number of projects underway. Each project is made up of various teams with members from different practices. As a Designer, you are part of the User-Centred Design practice.

    Learn about:

    • HM Land Registry Projects
    • User-Centred Design Disciplines/Roles
    • Digital, Data and Technology (DDaT)

  3. Step 3 Introduction to GOV.UK Design System

    User-Centred Design at HM Land Registry follows the principles outlined by the GOV.UK Design System. The Government Digital Service (GDS) Team are responsible for maintaining the GOV.UK Design System.

    Learn about:

    • The role and importance of a design system within UK Government digital services
    • How to use the GOV.UK Design System

  4. Step 4 Set up digital design software

    At HM Land Registry you are free to use the tools that enable you to complete the job required. However, we recommend using the following pieces of software to enable easier cross-team working.

    For prototyping and wireframing, we recommend using Figma or Adobe XD. Speak with fellow designers on your assigned projects to learn what prototyping software they are currently using.

    You may need a licence to use the software. To get a licence, please contact either your Line Manager, Head of Design or make a Marvel Request.

    If you are new to Figma or Adobe XD, we recommend completing the tutorials that appear when you open the software for the first time and any additional tutorial resources that are readily available.

    After you have downloaded the prototyping design software and completed the tutorials provided on the respective websites, download the HM Land Registry Design System Tool Kit for your chosen software.

    Download the HM Land Registry prototyping design toolkit for:

    • Figma - v1.0
    • Adobe XD - v1.0

  5. Step 5 Set up coded prototyping environment

    Designing and building prototypes is an important role for a Designer. While static prototypes can be built in Figma or Adobe XD, the HMLR Prototype Kit can be used to build fully interactive prototypes.

    Follow these guides on how to download:

    • GDS Prototype Kit
    • Atom code editor or Visual Studio Code

    Learn about:

    • Getting started with HTML, CSS, and Javascript
    • Introduction to code editors

    Complete the:

    • Tutorial on how to use the Prototype Kit

  6. Step 6 Begin the onboarding process to your project team

    Once you are officially assigned to a project, you will be inducted to the project by a member of the team. They will provide information on the project background, current status of work, as well as timelines and goals.

    In addition to explaining your role within the project and the associated expectations, the team member responsible for your onboarding will outline the responsibilities of other team members. This should ensure you have a clear understanding of where your role fits within the agile environment.

    Once you have been invited to all necessary meetings, you will be introduced to each team member. This will be an opportunity for team members to explain their role firsthand.

    Throughout the onboarding process, your team members may provide you with useful resources and materials to enable you to familiarise yourself with the work and build upon any skills that will be required.

  7. Step 7 Explore how HM Land Registry can contribute to your professional development

    At some point in your probationary period, alongside your Line Manager, you will write objectives for your first year at HM Land Registry to help identify the skills you wish to develop. To aid in this development, your Line Manager may suggest various training courses and resources of use.

    Your Line Manager will also explain the routes for progression and what metrics are used to assess readiness for promotion.
